What is Prim's Algorithm? MST

Prim's Algorithm finds the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) of a weighted undirected graph — a subset of edges that connects all nodes with the minimum total cost and no cycles. It was designed by Czech mathematician Vojtěch Jarník (1930) and popularized by Robert Prim (1957).

Think of it as: "I want to lay the least amount of cable to connect every building on campus." The algorithm greedily picks the cheapest edge that expands the growing tree, one node at a time.

Algorithm Steps

1
Pick Start

Choose any starting node. Mark it as visited.

2
Find Min Edge

Look at all edges from visited nodes to unvisited nodes.

3
Add to MST

Pick the minimum cost edge. Add node & edge to MST.

4
Repeat

Repeat steps 2–3 until all nodes are visited.

🌐

Network Design

Used by ISPs and campus IT teams to lay fiber or WiFi access points with minimum cabling cost, connecting all buildings efficiently.

🗺️

Road & Power Grids

City planners use MST to design road networks and electrical grids that reach all areas with the least infrastructure cost.

🧬

Bioinformatics

Prim's is used to reconstruct evolutionary trees (phylogenetics) and cluster genes based on similarity scores.

Our Campus WiFi Problem — What Are We Solving?

Imagine a university campus with 6 key buildings: Library, Dorm, Cafeteria, Lab, Gym, and Admin Block. The campus IT team needs to install WiFi repeater cables between these buildings. Each edge weight represents the signal routing cost (in hundreds of dollars) between two buildings. We want to connect all 6 buildings spending the least total cost possible. Prim's Algorithm will find the optimal set of cables — the Minimum Spanning Tree. Watch how it starts from the Library, greedily picks the cheapest neighboring connection, and expands until every building is connected!
